The 2016 Aston Martin DB11 Coupe

New Technology

In 2016, Aston Martin introduced the DB11 Coupe, a grand tourer that redefined the brand’s signature blend of performance and luxury. It replaced the DB9 updating its sleek design, with that iconic low-slung body and bold front grille, effortlessly capturing the classic Aston Martin spirit while bringing something fresh to the table.

Performance and Power

Under the hood, the DB11 houses a 5.2-liter twin-turbo V12 engine capable of generating 600 horsepower, allowing the car to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just under four seconds. This powerful engine not only delivers blistering speed but also offers smooth, effortless handling, which is enhanced by adaptive damping and a sophisticated chassis design.

Exotic Features

Whether you're cruising through city streets or pushing the car to its limits on the open highway, the DB11 offers unparalleled driving pleasure. The car’s multiple driving modes—Grand Tourer, Sport, and Sport+—allow drivers to fine-tune the performance to match road conditions and personal preference.

Luxury and Comfort

The interior of the DB11 is a work of art, with hand-stitched leather, real wood, and carbon fiber elements combining to create a cabin that exudes class and sophistication. Every inch of the interior has been carefully designed to ensure maximum comfort for the driver and passengers.

The advanced infotainment system, which was developed in collaboration with Daimler, features a 360-degree camera, intuitive controls, and a sleek display. Whether you're listening to your favorite tunes or navigating tricky roads, the DB11's technology makes every drive enjoyable and stress-free.

Bond Car

James Bond and Aston Martin have become synonymous with one another. The partnership has elevated Aston Martin’s status, making it a symbol of British sophistication, luxury, and high-performance vehicles. The DB11 continued this tradition, embodying the suave, thrilling nature of the world’s most famous secret agent.

The DB11's sleek design, powerful performance, and aura of exclusivity make it the perfect choice for a modern-day Bond. It's easy to imagine the British secret agent speeding down winding mountain roads or making a quick getaway in the DB11.
